# NexRemote Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 5, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how NexRemote by Neural Nexus Studios handles data when you use the packaged Windows desktop application and compatible clients.

1. Local-First Design

NexRemote is designed for local device-to-device control. Neural Nexus Studios does not require account registration, sign-in, analytics SDKs, advertising identifiers, or cloud-hosted telemetry for core local operation.

2. Data Stored Locally

The Windows app stores configuration, device identity, trusted-device records, logs, and certificate material on the local PC. These files are used to maintain pairing continuity, server configuration, and troubleshooting history.

3. Data Sent Across Your Network

When you start the local server and approve a device, NexRemote may transmit remote input commands, screen frames, camera frames, file-management requests, task-management requests, and related protocol messages between your own devices. These transfers are part of the product's core functionality.

4. Sensitive Capabilities

NexRemote asks for consent before enabling features that may affect privacy or security, including camera access, background availability, or remote control listeners. If consent is not granted, those features should remain disabled.

5. Certificates And Security

The desktop host uses local certificate material for secure transport. Certificate continuity matters for client trust, so local certificate files may be migrated from earlier NexRemote installations to preserve secure pairing behavior.

6. No Sale Or Advertising Use

Neural Nexus Studios does not sell personal information collected by NexRemote and does not use the app for advertising profiles, third-party analytics, or cross-app tracking.

7. Your Controls

You can review permissions, remove trusted devices, disconnect active clients, revoke background behavior, and delete the application with its local data. Windows privacy settings may also block camera access independently of the app.

8. Store And Platform Compliance

Where Microsoft Store, Windows, or other platform rules require capability declarations or user-facing privacy controls, NexRemote is intended to rely on those platform mechanisms rather than bypass them.

9. Contact

For privacy questions about NexRemote by Neural Nexus Studios, use the contact route published with the release, distribution page, or store listing for the version you installed.
