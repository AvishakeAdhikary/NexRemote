# NexRemote Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 8, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how NexRemote by Neural Nexus Studios handles information when you use the Windows host application and compatible client applications.

## 1. Product Scope

NexRemote is designed for local and user-approved remote control between devices that you own, administer, or are expressly authorized to manage. Core product features are intended to run directly between your devices instead of through a Neural Nexus Studios cloud account.

## 2. Information NexRemote Stores On Your Device

The Windows host may store the following data locally on the PC:

- App settings such as PC name, ports, theme preference, and local consent choices
- Trusted-device records and device identifiers used for pairing continuity
- Secure certificate material and certificate fingerprints used for local encrypted transport
- Logs and diagnostic records used for troubleshooting and auditing
- Legal acceptance timestamps and document-version acknowledgements

The Android client may store device identity, connection preferences, accepted legal notices, and other local app settings on the phone or tablet.

## 3. Information NexRemote Sends Between Your Devices

When you enable the server and approve a connection, NexRemote may transmit data directly between your devices over your local network or another connection path you choose, including:

- Device names, device identifiers, and connection metadata used for pairing
- Remote input commands such as keyboard, mouse, touch, or gamepad actions
- Screen-sharing frames and display metadata
- Camera-streaming frames and camera metadata
- File explorer, task manager, media-control, and clipboard requests and responses

These transfers are part of the product's core operation and are not intended to be sold, profiled for advertising, or shared with third-party analytics providers by Neural Nexus Studios.

## 4. Capabilities And Sensitive Features

NexRemote can access sensitive capabilities only when those capabilities are declared for the app and enabled by you or by the platform, such as:

- Local network communication for discovery, pairing, and remote control
- Webcam access for camera enumeration and live camera streaming
- Background and tray behavior so the host can remain available while the main window is hidden
- Clipboard, task-management, file-management, and screen-sharing features when you actively use them

If you do not grant the required consent, or if Windows privacy settings block a capability, the related feature should remain unavailable.

## 5. Security

NexRemote uses local certificate material to help protect secure transport between trusted devices. Certificate continuity matters for trust decisions, so certificate data may be preserved or migrated locally between compatible NexRemote installations on the same PC.

No security system is perfect. You are responsible for reviewing approved devices, removing devices you no longer trust, and protecting physical and account access to devices that run NexRemote.

## 6. No Sale, No Advertising, No Analytics Profiling

Neural Nexus Studios does not sell personal information collected by NexRemote, does not use NexRemote for cross-app advertising, and does not require analytics SDKs or advertising identifiers for the product's core local functionality.

## 7. Your Choices And Controls

You can:

- Review and change local settings inside the app
- Remove trusted devices
- Disconnect active clients
- Stop the server
- Revoke background use by changing settings or closing the app
- Use Windows privacy settings to restrict capabilities such as webcam access
- Uninstall the app and remove its local data if you no longer want to use it

## 8. Store Listing And Public Contact Information

The current public privacy-policy URL and support contact for NexRemote are expected to be published in the Microsoft Store listing, release page, or distribution page for the version you install. If a Store listing or distribution page includes more specific support or privacy contact information, that public listing should be treated as the current contact route for that release.

## 9. Contact

For privacy questions about NexRemote, use the public support or privacy contact published with the Microsoft Store listing, release page, or distribution page for your installed version.
